The easiest way to get promoted isn't by doing good work and hoping you're seen. It's by having this conversation with your boss, their skip-level, and a peer or two:

"My hope is to be considered for xyz role when another one opens, and I'd be grateful for your feedback. 

Do you think I'm ready for the role now, or are their skill set gaps I can be working on over the next year to ensure I have the best shot at it? What can I do today to make sure I'm as strong of a candidate as I can be?"

Speak early and loudly about your goals, be open to what you have to work on, and ensure your sponsors and mentors are looped in, too, so they can keep their ears out and advocate for you as well.

Bonus: you may not like or even trust the feedback you get from your leader, which is why those mentors and sponsors are key. They can either give you more grounded advice, or reinforce what you received, even if you didn't like it.
