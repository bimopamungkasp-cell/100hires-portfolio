I met my husband's friends for the first time in 2016 at a party. A BOLD choice for a third date, but I digress...

We left and Ryan said, "They LOVED you!" 

As peachy as I occasionally am, I said, "I know why - it's because I spent the night getting to know them and asking questions about their lives."

It's a super power of mine and while occasionally alarming to some, my love of asking questions has served me in my personal life, but so much so in sales.

NPR did a great bit on the likeability of people who ask questions, but...

...how they are exponentially more likeable if they ask active listening questions. 

And, Forrester found that the number one thing B2B buyers want from their sellers is: 

Active listeners. Someone who asks a question, truly listens, and asks another based on the answer they heard (read: not one that is self-serving or qualifying).

So, if we know that asking active listening questions makes us more likeable, and that people buy from people they like, more often than not, it's a home run for all our sellers.

But the question is: where does it stack up on our training priorities? And have you considered the generational changes in how a 23 yr old communicates vs. a 53 yr old? It may come naturally to one and feel invasive to another. 

Seller, BDR, accountant, wealth manager, CSM...we can level up every person that engages with our buyers with just one tweak, and by teaching the art of great conversation.
