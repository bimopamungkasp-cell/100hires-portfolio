A year ago, I woke up at 4am.

I thought I heard the front door closing.

I walked downstairs and saw my 3-year-old son outside in the dark. He had learned to open the deadbolt and was proudly smiling at me through the window like, "Look Dada, I can open the door now!"

Scared the hell out of me.

(We bought all the contraptions to baby-proof the doors. But… I kept thinking, What If…)

This morning, I went to wake him up. He's usually up early and hungry.

He wasn't in his bed. Or in the bathroom. Our room. The hallway.

I panicked.

I remember the taste of what could only be described as "fear" in my mouth (a mix of saliva and rust?) and tried to put a plan together with my heart lodged in my throat.

In an instant, all my day’s priorities immediately and violently shifted.

Then he came out from behind the bathroom door. “Surpirise!” 

I dropped to my knees and cried like a child.

What does this have to do with your sales strategy, your outreach, your pitch?

Maybe everything.

Value explains decisions. Emotion makes them.

Yes, I’m aware that adding emotion can backfire (e.g. "Act fast before it’s too late!”). 

But using emotion is often under-done. Be honest, if I looked at your copy right now, you're probably not making your prospects feel enough to engage.

Even your most analytical prospects: the engineers, the CFOs, the "show me the data!" types… they're still people. And there's still a person behind every transaction who wants to feel something. Confidence. Relief. Excitement. The fear of getting it wrong.

So try this: 

Before you hit send, ask yourself, “How will this message make my prospect feel?”
