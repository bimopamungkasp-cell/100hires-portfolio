We had a client paying $50,000 a year for ZoomInfo, nobody on their team ever logged in.

They didn't buy a bad tool. They bought a tool with no one driving it.

The license that you purchase is not the outcome, the outcome comes from a person doing the work with it.

And right now everyone is making the same mistake with AI.

Founders buy an agent, point it at the whole GTM motion, and wait for pipeline. It doesn't come. Replies go silent, copy sounds like a robot wrote it, and they decide AI doesn't work.

AI works. It was just never going to run the whole thing on its own.

We've run outbound for 276+ companies now. We use the same rule on every one. I call it 10-80-10.

You own the first 10%. Who we go after, what a good lead looks like, the angle to take. That's judgment, and no model makes that call for you. It's also the part people often skip.

AI runs the middle 80%. The research, the data, the first drafts, the follow-ups. Hundreds of accounts in a night, across the tools you already pay for. We build our own software on top so it keeps getting better at that 80%.

Then you own the last 10%. You read the work before a prospect ever does. You catch what the model got wrong, cut what's weak, and decide what goes out.

We've never let an agent run a play end to end. There's always a human on both ends of it.

I don't think AI is coming for your team. I think it makes a good team faster, and it does close to nothing for a team that couldn't tell good work from bad in the first place.

Once AI can do the 80% for everyone, doing the work stops being the edge. The 20% that decides what's worth chasing and what's good enough to send becomes the whole game.

That 20% is what clients pay us for. It's the reason this still needs people.
