Turn the people engaging with your LinkedIn posts into booked meetings.

Here's the 7-step workflow that got our team 120 meetings last month.

1/ Post for your ICP

We use Taplio to spot trending topics before we write, and to see which of our own posts pull the most attention. Then we double down on what's working.

2/ Pull everyone who engaged.

Someone who reacts to a post about outbound automation is telling you what they care about. We use Apify to export a clean list of every profile that liked, commented, or reposted.


3/ Enrich the list in Clay.

We push the list into Clay and layer in company, role, seniority, and firmographics. Now it's a real prospecting dataset instead of a list of names.

4/ Score and tier by ICP fit.

Not everyone who engages is a buyer. We rank each account in Clay. 
Tier 1 is a strong match on role, company size, and industry. Tier 2 and 3 fit, but with lower urgency. 
This keeps us from burning touches on people who were never going to buy.

5/ Find verified emails with a waterfall.

We run the enrichment in sequence inside Clay: Wiza, then Prospeo, then LeadMagic, then FullEnrich. 
If one tool misses the email, the next one catches it. Coverage stays high and you never overpay a single provider.

6/ Add buying signals before you write anything.

This is where relevance comes from. Before reaching out, we pull live context in Clay: active hiring, tech stack, team growth. 
A company hiring SDRs is a completely different conversation than one that isn't.

7/ Reach out based on tier.

Tier 1 gets a LinkedIn DM through Instantly.ai or Expandi, more personal and higher intent. 
Tier 2 and 3 get an email sequence through Instantly, scaled but still personalized with the signals from step 6.

The one rule we never break: no pitch on the first touch. We open with something useful instead, a relevant lead magnet, a quick insight, or the results we got for a similar company. We show we understand their situation before we ask for anything.

Engagement is just the top of the funnel. This workflow is how you turn it into pipeline.
