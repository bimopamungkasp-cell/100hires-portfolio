We wired 19 APIs into Claude Code to run our entire GTM motion across 7 layers, from first signal to revenue.

Pay for 10+ tools that never share data and someone becomes the integration layer by hand. Claude Code connects all of it.

1️⃣ Signal layer: where intent shows up

PredictLeads tracks hiring surges, funding rounds, and tech adoption across 100M+ companies. It shows up here before anyone fills out a form.

Common Room pulls community, product, and GTM signals into one place. It surfaces warm leads from GitHub, Discord, LinkedIn, G2, and job changes.

Attention records every sales call, then lets you query the history in plain language for competitor mentions and objections.

RB2B / Vector deanonymizes your website visitors. People already on your site are warmer than any cold list you'll ever buy.

2️⃣ Data layer: where contacts get enriched

Openmart holds 200M+ local business records. Search by category and location, enrich with verified emails and phones.

Wiza turns a LinkedIn profile into verified contact data, 50+ points per person.

Prospeo runs at 98%+ email accuracy across a 200M+ database, with person, company, and domain search.

FullEnrich is the waterfall play: one API cascades through 20+ providers until it finds the email and phone, 80%+ find rates. It replaced 3 enrichment subscriptions for most teams we work with.

Apollo covers B2B contact and company data, with firmographic and technographic filters.

3️⃣ Action layer: where outreach runs itself

The part most teams still do by hand. Claude Code creates the campaigns, triggers the sequences, and manages follow-ups without anyone touching a UI.

Instantly gives you programmatic control over cold email: campaigns, warmup, analytics, and webhooks that fire on replies.

Lemlist runs multichannel sequences across email, LinkedIn, and calls, routing each lead by enrichment data.

LinkedIn Ads handles paid acquisition: create campaigns, sync audiences, retarget accounts already showing intent.

4️⃣ Automation layer: where it all connects

n8n orchestrates everything above into multi-step workflows that trigger, branch, and loop. This is the glue.

5️⃣ System of record

Attio is the AI-native CRM holding it together: people, companies, deals, custom objects, lists, tasks, all with real-time webhooks.

6️⃣ Conversion layer

Cal.com handles scheduling: embed booking links in emails, auto-route meetings, sync every booking back to the CRM.

7️⃣ Revenue layer

Hyperline closes the loop: billing, subscriptions, usage metering, invoicing, plus webhooks on payments and ARR/MRR/churn analytics.

Once it's connected, you can run:

Full-cycle outbound where signal becomes enrichment becomes outreach becomes a CRM update, with no manual steps in between.

GTM agents that monitor signals, enrich contacts, route to the right channel, and report back around the clock.

The 19 tools were never the advantage. Connecting them is. Which one would you wire up first?
