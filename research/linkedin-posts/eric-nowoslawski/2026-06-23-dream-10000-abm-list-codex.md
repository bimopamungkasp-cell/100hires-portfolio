I built an ABM list for TheHog .ai as a part of a Dream 10,000 experiment I ran in Codex yesterday. Here's what I'm thinking and how I built it.

Chet Holmes famously created the idea of the Dream 100 list. You find 100 perfect customers and do anything you can to break into those accounts.

But it got me thinking, the work I would do manually for these dream 100 companies can also be packaged into skills and done with AI now.

Why can't we have a dream 10,000 list instead now?

So as experiment, I pulled a list of companies that are hiring for GTM Engineers and told Codex, "use the skills we have built around list building, validation, and ICP prompting to fulfill samples of what these open jobs will be responsible for."

The Hog was the first one and the job description specifically asked for ABM lists.

So without any other prompting from me, Codex found 100 perfect fit companies that could integrate an AI research agent into their product.

It created columns like what messaging should we use to break in, who are the best contacts, and trigger signals to monitor each account for.

Taking the use cases as an example, it successfully delineated that Clay could use thehog to power their waterfall enrichments/claygent and suggested the hog could power the account research for 11x to give the AI SDR better perception of the company before automatically reaching out.

It didn't just give the same suggestion for each. It had real ABM use case messaging.

And I also threw this together as an example for a YouTube video and the quality was so much higher than I thought.

We are definitely going to fine tune this more and create Dream 10,000 outbound campaigns with it.
