We saved a client from a 0.41% to a 3.34% reply rate in 15 days.

Only one of the five changes had anything to do with infrastructure. The rest was strategy.

Here's the whole list:

1. Super spintax. 20 variations in the subject line, the unsubscribe language, and the signature block. Not just a little bit of spintax, far more than we thought we would need.

2. Buy domains early. A parked, aged domain beats a fresh one you warmed for two weeks.

We keep 50-100% extra inbox capacity warming as insurance with live inboxes. Another 50-100% of the domains we need just sitting in the registrar.

3. 100% Gmail. We buy Gmails through Zapmail and Hypertide, and we added Zapmail's ZapShield, which puts inboxes on Google Cloud enterprise servers.

We split-tested it: a 20-50% reply-rate lift depending on the campaign.

4. Find the winners, pile on. We run two inboxes per domain usually. If one domain is proving to be particularly strong by reply rate then stack up to five on the outlier domains getting the best reply rate. (Credit to Felipe Fuhr for this one.)

5. Kill anything under 1%. Every week I pull a report and cut about 10-15% of our infrastructure. Every inbox lives in a database as active, insurance, or cancel with the trailing 7 day reply rate.

More copywriting variance, more infrastructure, bought ahead of time.

That's the whole game right now.
