Early in my sales career, I was the turbulence.

Bad meeting?
I was the frustration.

Prospect ghosts?
I was the rejection.

Behind quota?
I was the fear.

I didn’t experience those feelings.

I became them.

Then I learned something that changed everything.

Feelings are weather.

They come.

They go.

Pilots don’t fight turbulence.

They don’t take it personally.

They don’t start yanking the controls.

They notice it.

They make small adjustments.

And they keep flying.

Same thing in sales.

Fear shows up.

Notice it.

Frustration shows up.

Notice it.

Doubt shows up.

Notice it.

Then remind yourself:

“I am the pilot, not the turbulence.”

You don’t have to get rid of the weather.

You just have to remember who’s flying the plane.
