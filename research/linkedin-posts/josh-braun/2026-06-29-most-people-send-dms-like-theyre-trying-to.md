Most people send DMs like they're trying to book a meeting.

You don't need to.

I got this message recently:

Seller:
"Josh, been following you for a few years. Is all your business generated via LinkedIn or do you do outbound?"

Me:
"Mostly inbound. On occasion I'll do some outbound for targeted accounts."

Seller:
"Made you a 2-minute video showing commenters who might be a fit for your workshop so you can reach out. Open to seeing it?"

 Me:
"Sure."

Why it worked:

They got me talking first.

Then they offered a meaningfully different idea tied to a problem I actually have.

Most importantly, they didn't ask me to book a meeting.

They let the idea do the work.

A two-minute video lets me decide for myself whether it's interesting.

No calendar.
No commitment.
No awkward "discovery call."

Just enough information to decide if it's worth taking the next step.

The less friction there is to seeing your idea...
the more likely people are to see it.
