A bad cold call is just a bad cold call.

A bad haircut is just a bad haircut.

A bad sandwich is just a bad sandwich.

The problem isn’t the bad experience.

It’s being surprised by it.

Somehow we expect every cold call to go well.
Every haircut to be perfect.
Every sandwhich to be amazing.

But good and bad are part of the same experience.

You don’t get one without occasionally getting the other.

When you stop expecting good, “bad” loses its power.
They’re no longer evidence that something is wrong.
They’re simply one of the bad ones.
