Your prospect doesn’t have a problem when you reach out.

They’re getting the job done.

They’re making coffee.

Drip machine, Keurig, espresso.

It works.
They’re happy.

To get their attention, you have to shine a light on a problem they don’t know they have.

Make the status quo feel unsafe.

You have the poke the bear. 

Like this:

Most coffee makers run hot water through plastic tubes, valves, reservoirs, and brew baskets before it ever hits your cup.

Heat can speed up how much stuff leaches from some plastics.

A few studies have found microplastics coming off those parts. The health impact is still being studied.

And now your brain is curious.

You’re wondering, Hmm… should I look into this?

What happened?

You didn’t sell a French press.

You made them look at something they use every single morning and feel a little less safe about it.

That’s how you create demand where there wasn’t any.

Not by talking about your product.

By making the status quo feel risky.

Curiosity makes people scratch their head and think “Hmm, that’s interesting. I’m not sure.”

It’s not your job to talk people into buying. 

Your job is to hold the flashlight.

People don’t resist change.
They resist being changed.
